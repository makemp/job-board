What’s the role

The Instrument Sub-System Lead is accountable for availability, arrangement of any necessary service or support contracts, and lifecycle management of Qatar Shell Gas To Liquid (QSGTL) PLC-based Control and Safeguarding Systems (Sub-Systems). In QSGTL, there are 25 Sub-Systems deployed offshore and onshore process areas, and the office buildings.

The Instrument Sub-System Lead must maintain skill-level competence in primary role dimensions and act as the ultimate Subject Matter Expert in PLC-based Control Automation and Safeguarding Technologies present in QSGTL. This role is a Line Manager for the Sub-System team with diverse team members consisting of Shell staff and Direct contractors.

You will have the opportunity to thrive in:

Complex Operations Management: Oversee daily administration and lifecycle activities of a large, intricate Sub-Systems landscape while maintaining reliability and availability.

Cross-Functional Coordination: Act as the primary interface across diverse teams—Production Operations, Engineering, Technology, IDT, and vendors—navigating varied work cultures and dependencies.

Operational Continuity: Execute work within a live plant environment without disrupting production, relying on multiple contractors and remote support organizations.

What you’ll be doing
You will be accountable for the following role dimensions:

Sub-Systems Control System Custodian – maintain PLC-based Control and Safeguarding systems (Sub-Systems), accountable for Sub-Systems overall availability and arrangement of any necessary service or support contracts, manage Lifecycle Upgrade activities and oversee Project funnel for Sub-Systems.

Ensure Sub-System Reliability: Oversee maintenance strategies, inspections, and performance monitoring to minimize downtime and optimize operations.

Manage Contracts & Budgets: Handle R&M and project contracts with OEM vendors, ensuring performance and budget compliance.

Support Strategic Maintenance Planning: Assist in developing preventive, predictive, and corrective maintenance programs, including Honeywell A360 and turnaround lifecycle planning.

Drive Risk & Obsolescence Management: Conduct root cause investigations, manage obsolescence risks, and implement mitigation strategies for aging equipment.

Ensure Compliance & Security: Support deployment of QSGTL PCD-I Program, manage asset inventory, access control, and ensure alignment with OEM and internal standards.

Technical Authority - QSGTL Instrument Sub-System Lead must maintain Process Automation Control and Optimization (PACO) TA-3 competencies or be ready to get PACO TA-3 assessment.

Support business ability to make quality risk-based decisions.

Assure as necessary design and effectiveness of QSGTL Sub-Systems controls.

Evaluate QSGTL Sub-Systems related changes as part of its Management of Change process.

Identify alternative risk reduction methods, mitigations, and considerations.

Line Manager – Instrument Sub-System Lead is responsible for management of Instrument Sub-System team consisting of Shell staff and Direct Contractors.

Team Leadership & Development: Lead the Sub-System team, ensuring competency, mentoring, and career growth through succession planning and training.

Operational Coordination: Align team activities with QSGTL goals to ensure efficient and effective Sub-System operations.

Compliance & Standards: Ensure adherence to QSGTL’s HSSE, HR, PTW, MOC, and Qatarization processes while promoting technical standards and best practices.

Strategic Alignment: Translate instrumentation strategy into actionable Sub-System roadmaps and drive continuous improvement using data.

Culture & Agility: Foster a collaborative, high-performance culture, manage conflicts, and implement agile practices for team adaptability.

What you bring

Possessing these qualifications is considered a significant asset and may enhance a candidate’s effectiveness and success in the role:

Bachelor’s in Control Automation & Instrumentation Engineering with 10+ years in large-scale industrial environments; professional accreditations are a plus.

Proven ability to manage control systems and lead technical teams across various PLC platforms (e.g., Siemens, GE, HIMA).

Project & Contract Management: Background in managing engineering projects and vendor contracts; FEED and Detailed Design.

Deep understanding of automation system design, scientific principles, and interdependent technologies.

Communication & Leadership: Effective communicator with leadership capabilities to mentor, motivate, and collaborate across teams.

Reliability Engineering: Experienced in applying RCA, FMEA, and risk-based approaches to enhance system reliability.

Hands-On Technical Skills: Proficient in instrumentation assembly, loop testing, network validation, and smart instrumentation integration.

Documentation & Standards: Capable of preparing and evaluating engineering documents including wiring diagrams, control narratives, and network architectures.

Problem Solving & Analysis: Strong analytical skills for diagnosing complex issues and implementing effective solutions.

Process Knowledge: Skilled in Production Operations processes (AMS, SAP, MOC, PtW, etc.) and lifecycle management.

It is a Site based position requiring daily travel to site (Ras Laffan) and visible site presence.
Compliance to Company’s code of conduct, Procedures and Permit-To-Work System are mandatory requirements.
